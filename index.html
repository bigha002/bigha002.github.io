About Me

I am a Junior at Michigan Technological University where I study Robotics Engineering with a minor in Mathematical Sciences. I have a deep passion for engineering, specifically spacefaring technology!

Currently, I serve as a research engineer at the competitive Planetary Surface Technology Development Laboratory (also known as Huskyworks), a technology accelerator specialized for in-situ resource utilization. My area of expertise in the lab is power management engineering, avionics, and microcontrollers. I headed the development of our team's custom battery management system submission to NASA's Watts on the Moon challenge in 2024. In order to succeed in Huskyworks as a sophomore engineer, I had to learn quickly and work hard!

As an engineer, I stay plenty busy when not at work or school! I have a rich portfolio of solo projects to hone my skills or generate income. I'm even in the midst of the patenting process for one of my more useful ideas! Take a look at some of these projects further down the page.

For my mathematical sciences minor, I study coding theory for communication with objects at astronomical distances (think martian rovers or deep-space probes like Voyager). I have an intimate knowledge of advanced numerical linear algebra and differential equations.
